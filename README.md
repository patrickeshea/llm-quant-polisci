# LLM Tools for Quantitative Research in Political and Social Sciences

Materials for the workshop session *AI and Quantitative Methods: LLMs and 
Quantitative Research in Social and Political Sciences*, University of Glasgow, 
22 April 2026.

**Patrick E. Shea**  
Senior Lecturer, International Political Economy & Development  
School of Social and Political Sciences, University of Glasgow  
patrick.shea@glasgow.ac.uk

---

## Contents

The session document covers four topics:

- LLM-assisted coding in R (GitHub CoPilot, `ellmer`, Claude Code)
- Text-as-data applications using LLMs (`quallmer`)
- Choosing between open and closed models
- Validation, reproducibility, and researcher judgment

The document covers more than the session itself — it is designed to function 
as a reference guide after the session.

## Note on scope

This session covers LLM tools for quantitative research workflows. It does 
not cover writing assistance, literature review tools, teaching applications, 
or ethics debates — those are separate conversations.
